Frequently Asked Questions

What equipment does Hudson Valley Powerlifting have?

We have competition-grade powerlifting equipment identical to USAPL national meets, including Texas Strength Systems combo racks, calibrated competition plates from Texas Strength Systems and Rogue, competition benches, deadlift platforms, and a full range of specialty bars.

Is Hudson Valley Powerlifting open 24/7?

Yes. Members have 24/7 key fob access, 365 days a year. Train whenever fits your schedule—early morning, late night, or holidays.

Do you offer powerlifting coaching?

Yes. We offer coaching for all levels, from beginners learning squat, bench, and deadlift technique to experienced lifters preparing for competition. In-person and remote programming options available.

Where is HVPL located?

We're at 155 Vineyard Ave in Highland, NY—minutes from the Mid-Hudson Bridge, with easy access from Highland, Poughkeepsie, Newburgh, New Paltz, and Kingston. We serve lifters across Ulster and Dutchess counties throughout the Mid-Hudson Valley.

Can I try the gym before joining?

Absolutely. We offer day passes and drop-in options. Contact us to schedule a visit or just stop by.

What's the difference between HVPL and a regular gym?

We're built specifically for powerlifting and serious strength training. No waiting for squat racks, no restrictions on chalk or deadlifts, competition-calibrated equipment, and a focused community of lifters who support each other.

Do I need to be experienced to join?

No. We welcome all levels. Many members started as beginners and learned proper technique here. Our coaching services are great for those new to powerlifting.
